# λ-robust verify-tree topology — can a different tree lower the self-KV-recovery bar? (PR #184)

## TL;DR — the hypothesis mechanism is refuted; bank the negative

denken #178 reframed the launch around the self-KV-recovery fraction **λ**: at the realistic
liveprobe anchor **λ̂≈0.342** both the deployed #83 tree and the descent-only path miss 500.
#83 was optimised for max-E[T]-at-λ=1; this PR asked the *contingency* question — does a
**different** 32-node max-branch-3 topology clear 500 at a **lower** recovery bar λ_bar than
#83's both-bugs **0.838**? The hypothesis MECHANISM was to **front-load** acceptance into the
shallow, λ-insensitive depths (depth-1 is pinned λ-insensitive in the both-bugs model).

> **Front-loading is REFUTED.** Shallow/wide trees are strictly *worse*: at the measured depth-7
> horizon **no** 32-node max-branch-3 tree clears 500 even at full recovery (λ=1). The bar λ_bar
> is **dominated by E[T]@λ=1** (corr(E[T]@1, λ_bar) = **−0.95**) because the λ=0 floor E[T]@0 is
> near topology-invariant (~3.6) — so **min-λ-bar ≡ max-E[T]@λ=1**, the *same* lever #83 already
> optimised. The only trees that beat 0.838 do so via **more depth** (more E[T]@λ=1 headroom),
> the opposite of front-loading, and only by **extrapolating the recovery curve past its measured
> depth-7 horizon**. At the as-built depth-9 footprint **#83 is already the λ-robust optimum**
> (nothing beats 0.9052 LCB / 0.8384 central). And **no** topology clears 500 at the realistic
> λ̂=0.342 — the recovery gap is **2.4× λ̂**, far too large for the topology lever to close.

`VERDICT: BANK-NEGATIVE-ONLY-DEEP-EXTRAP-BEATS-83`. The contingency is **negative within the
defensible horizon**: topology cannot rescue the realistic floor; only the self-KV recovery fix
(land #71's built-kernel descent ladder) can.

## 3. Pareto frontier (λ_bar ↓, E[T]@λ=1 ↑) — the no-free-lunch structure

Because the λ=0 floor E[T]@0 is near topology-invariant (~3.6), λ_bar is ~a monotone function of
E[T]@1 alone (corr **−0.95**). The min-λ-bar and max-E[T]@1 objectives are **aligned**, so over
the bulk the "frontier" collapses to the deepest feasible tree. A genuine trade only appears at
the deep extreme, where pushing depth past the **knee (depth-15)** erodes the floor faster than it
raises the peak — `pareto_monotone = True` (6-point non-dominated frontier):

| frontier point | depth | E[T]@1 | E[T]@0 | central TPS@1 | λ_bar (LCB) | λ_bar (central) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| min-λ_bar | 14 | 5.5216 | 3.5747 | 567.8 | **0.8345** | 0.7766 |
| | 14 | 5.5273 | 3.5598 | 568.4 | 0.8351 | 0.7780 |
| **knee** | 15 | 5.5449 | 3.5506 | 570.2 | 0.8363 | 0.7799 |
| | 16 | 5.5507 | 3.5090 | 570.8 | 0.8434 | 0.7881 |
| | 17 | 5.5664 | 3.4904 | 572.4 | 0.8455 | 0.7912 |
| max-E[T]@1 | 18 | 5.5736 | 3.4686 | 573.1 | 0.8495 | 0.7962 |

**No free lunch:** `max E[T]@λ=0 = 3.7052 < bar 4.8624` for **every** topology in the family —
so λ_bar > 0 *always*. **No 32-node max-branch-3 tree can clear 500 at the floor (λ=0); topology
alone never substitutes for self-KV recovery.** Past the knee, +0.05 E[T]@1 (depth 15→18) costs
+0.013 λ_bar (the floor erodes 3.55→3.47) — the trade is real but tiny, and entirely inside the
extrapolated-depth regime.

## 4. Recommended-topology LCB, correlation-aware (closes #175's iid caveat)

#175's TPS CI assumed iid decode steps. The benchmark's `N_steps = 3147` steps are **not** iid:
**128 prompts** are independent but the ~**24.6 steps/prompt** within a prompt are correlated.
Design effect `Deff = 1 + (m̄−1)·ICC`, `N_eff = N_steps/Deff`, accept-length half-width × √Deff.
For the recommended topology (#83 at λ=1, the defensible operating point):

| ICC | Deff | N_eff | accept ± (TPS) | total ± (TPS) | LCB (TPS) | clears 500? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0.0 (iid, #175) | 1.00 | 3147 | 10.91 | 14.48 | 521.0 | **Yes** |
| 0.05 | 2.18 | 1444 | 16.10 | 18.71 | 516.7 | Yes |
| 0.10 | 3.36 | 937 | 19.99 | 22.14 | 513.3 | Yes |
| 0.30 | 8.07 | 390 | 30.99 | 32.42 | 503.0 | Yes |
| 0.50 | 12.79 | 246 | 39.01 | 40.15 | 495.3 | **No** |
| **1.0 (worst case)** | 24.58 | **128** | 54.07 | 54.91 | **480.5** | **No** |

`recommended_topology_lcb_clears_500` (worst-case ICC=1, N_eff=128) = **False**. #175's iid
clear-500 (LCB=521) **survives moderate clustering (ICC ≤ ~0.4) but flips under heavy
clustering** — the worst-case half-width inflates **3.8×** (±10.9 → ±54.9). This closes #175's
iid caveat: even #83 at *full* recovery has a fragile clear-500 margin once within-prompt
correlation is admitted, which strengthens the case that the binding lever is recovery, not
topology. (The depth-11 extrapolation tree behaves the same: worst-case LCB 492.4, also fails.)
